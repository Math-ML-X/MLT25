Overview


Machine learning is a driving force behind advancements in areas such as healthcare, finance, and automation. This course focuses on developing a strong understanding of the theoretical principles that form the foundation of machine learning, emphasising the reasoning behind algorithms rather than just their implementation. By studying concepts like generalisation, optimisation, and complexity, students will learn to analyse and design algorithms that effectively learn from data. The course leverage mathematical rigour with practical applications, covering topics such as PAC learning, kernel methods, neural networks, and optimisation. By the end of the course, students will be equipped with the knowledge to critically evaluate existing methods, design new solutions, and understand the limitations and potential of machine learning systems.
